Executive Snapshot

Purpose: Focus T4 DV intervention effort on the highest-risk, persistently active zones. Tiering drives resource allocation; downtrend does not remove a zone until validated. Top 50 persistent hotspots combined with 95%+ priority zones form the operational overlay. Community-reported incidents augment CAD/RMS signal but do not, on their own, tier a zone.

How to Read This Map

  1. Start with the citywide view to confirm cluster geography and district coverage.
  2. Cross-reference Priority Intervention Zones (95%+) against Persistent Hotspots (Top 50).
  3. Treat overlap of both layers as the strongest intervention signal.
  4. Use Community-Reported Incidents as corroborating context only.
  5. Verify District (Posts) and Beat alignment before tasking.

Legend Interpretation

LayerOperational Meaning
T4 Persistent Hotspots (Top 50)Locations with sustained DV activity across multiple cycles. Strongest candidate for directed patrol and follow-up.
T4 Priority Intervention Zones (95%+)Zones exceeding the 95th percentile risk threshold for the current cycle. Primary tasking targets.
T4 Community-Reported IncidentsNon-CAD sourced signals. Used for context and corroboration, not standalone tiering.
T4 Incidents Within City (Reference)All in-city DV incidents for the cycle. Provides denominator and coverage reference.
District (Posts)Command and tasking boundary. Drives responsibility assignment.
BeatPatrol-level boundary used for unit-level accountability within a District.
